Learning Plan

Success doesn't just happen, it's planned for

Long term goal or career pathway: My goal is to break into the tech sector with a Junior Software Development role and work my way up from there. At this stage I'm still exploring whether I'd be more interested in Front-end Development or Back-end Development - haven't yet dived into too much JavaScript yet but I have enjoyed both sides from what I have learnt so far in Dev Academy and previous to this course. I know it will be hard and there's a lot of competition in the tech sector but I'm determined to make it as a Software Developer.


Strengths and limitations when it comes to learning: Strengths: - I know how I learn best which is seeing demonstrations of something and then putting it into practice myself before moving onto the next learning objective - focussing on one thing at a time - I'm good at researching/googling and checking multiple resources before asking for help, this helps keep me more engaged with learning - When I put my mind to something, I'm determined to figure out the solution preferably without needing to ask for help Limitations: - I'm quiet and not so confident which I'll need to really focus on improving to get the best out of my Dev Academy experience - I can get distracted quite easily which is why I need to reduce distractions at home - I'm good at procrastinating and doing all the things that are less important first


Human skills I would like to develop during Dev Academy: I would like to become more confident at speaking up in group situations and would like to learn how to communicate with others more effectively. This has definitely been my main focus in terms of human skills coming into this course. I would also like to get to know myself a bit better in terms of values and what I want with my life which will come hand in hand with developing confidence and the ability to speak up.


Commitment to how I will manage my workload: I will manage my workload by sticking to a daily schedule as if I were working a full-time job still and using the pomodoro timer to make sure I have productive focus time and take breaks frequently to refresh my mind and increase engagement. Each day I will draft out a to do list so I know what tasks need to be completed daily and make sure I communicate my progress/any obstacles with team mates so everyone knows where I'm at. During this time I will also look after my own well-being by getting outside in the sun and going for walks daily, going to the gym/pilates 5x per week in the morning, eating foods that will fuel my learning, journaling daily, and try creating an effective evening wind down routine for a good nights sleep. When I'm starting to feel stress building up, I need to first make sure I have created a to do list so I know where I'm at with tasks and what I still need to complete. I may also need to take a break from the topic to go back with a fresh perspective or ask for help/support. It's also often helpful to just talk to someone or write down notes explaining where I am stuck and talk it out with myself as I may be able to solve the issue myself this way.


How and where I will seek help in a timely way: If I'm needing help with study related questions, there are a number of resources available to me with Dev Academy. There are multiple discord groups where I can seek help in a timely fashion from facilitators along with other groups where I can seek help from peers that may know the answers that I'm after.


What I expect from the facilitation team: To be available when we need help during week days and have our weekly catch ups so we can all check in together as a team. To also have the knowledge to help educate us on any problems that may pop up while we're learning coding basics and to just openly be available to reach out to when we're having trouble.


Scheduling information: The only commitments I do currently have involve gym, pilates and daily walks. Gym and pilates are to be completed in the morning before I start my study day and my walk is to be completed during my lunch break. I am currently giving myself 1 hour and 30 minutes for my lunch break so I have enough time to go for a long walk, shower, and have lunch. During the first two weeks I have struggled sticking to my schedule so I'm making some changes so I can stay on track better. My new and improved daily schedule from Monday-Friday will be focus time between 8am - 5pm with a lunch break between 11:30am - 1pm. I am going to set up alarms to keep me on track with this schedule and make sure I have a to do list prepared for what I need to get done in terms of both study and personal life each day. I also need to make sure I am getting my daily workouts in between 5am - 7am each morning so I have time to prepare for a productive day of study before 8am. I expect to be at my desk completing my reflection questions for the day at 8am.

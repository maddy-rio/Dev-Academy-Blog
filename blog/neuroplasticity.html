Exploring Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindsets


Neuroplasticity..


How the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people: Understanding the principles of neuroplasticity is really powerful because brain plasticity refers to the brains ability to adapt and change through experiences. This can be so effective for learning, mental health, and re-training your brain after an accident. A lot of people believe you’re born with a skill set or way of thinking and that’s final, whereas realistically your way of thinking and knowledge can be changed and moulded in so many positive ways with persistence and practice. I can link this to my self development journey over the last few years and know first hand how effective re-training your thinking and adapting to a growth mindset can be.


How you might engage with the principles of neuroplasticity for your benefit: An example of utilising neuroplasticity could be simply taking this Dev Academy course as a fresh student with no prior knowledge of coding. Each day I’m showing up and learning new skills and terms I haven’t previously heard of before. It’s hard work but the more practice, and focus I put into these new skills, the stronger this new neural pathway will become and be easier for my brain to recognise.


What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity? The best way to increase neuroplasticity is to practice what you’re trying to learn or the habit you’re trying to incorporate in your daily routine. The more often you repeat a certain behaviour, the stronger this new neural pathway gets. The opposite goes for behaviours that you no longer want to continue, the less you do these behaviours, the weaker that neural pathway gets. It’s also helpful to be aware of the behaviours or skills you want to build and how you’re going to achieve this for the most success.

I can also now see how neuroplasticity can be linked to learning and other areas of life. I didn’t actually know what the term neuroplasticity meant before this course but I have used this process to rewire my brain and way of thinking for the better so it’s great to be able to link it to a proper term.



Growth Mindset..


What is a growth mindset and why is it relevant? - Growth mindset is SO important in terms of everyday learning and how you react to different situations. Having a growth mindset means that when you face challenges and setbacks in life you believe that you can still achieve your goals and develop the skills you need to reach those goals - you believe that your abilities can be improved and aren’t permanent. Someone with a fixed mindset views challenges and setbacks in the opposite way and often thinks the challenges are too difficult to overcome or that they’re simply not smart enough and that’s how it is.


In this exploration, did anything surprise you? Change for you? - For the past while during my self-development journey I have become very aware of growth mindsets vs fixed mindsets and how important this is in terms of learning and how you view life. I think this is a great thing to be teaching in this course and it’s important that more people and adolescents in particular are educated on this as it’s something I really struggled with as a young kid. I was so caught up in having good grades as a kid and being afraid to fail that I would get really upset and give up if I couldn’t be perfect at school which is the perfect example of having a fixed mindset. It wasn’t until after high school that I started learning more about mindset and how it affects your well-being that I realised I can change my mindset to be more open and that would in turn improve my mental health too.


How will you integrate growth mindset into your learning journey? - Reminding myself all the time that there’s nothing wrong with the stage I’m at and the only person I should compare my learning to is my own. We’re all on different learning journeys and have started at different points and should be proud of where we’re all at right now. A lot of what I need is that positive self talk to remind myself that I can do this and the challenges are all part of the learning journey here. Even just writing this I’m realising that I have been more caught up and anxious about the future but I need to enjoy the learning I’m doing right now because this is all exciting.


Useful resource: Growth Mindset Ted Talk - This is the resource used in the course but I had already watched this video previous to starting Dev Academy I guess to hype myself up as I was quitting my job and jumping into something completely new. It’s honestly amazing how powerful mindset changes can be and this part of the course came at the perfect time to remind me to be more present and believe in myself through this course.



Reflection..


How will these understandings shape or influence your learning plan and strategy? - Having a better understanding on neuroplasticity and growth mindset has been great and the refresher I needed to make sure I’m supporting myself and believing in myself through this process. The better your mindset, the easier it is to achieve what you need to do. It has also reminded me that the more I practice the new habits I’m trying to implement, the easier they will become.

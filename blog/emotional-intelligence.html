Emotional Intelligence

Discussing my understanding of EQ vs IQ

What is Emotional Intelligence? Emotional Intelligence is the ability to manage and understand your own emotions and the emotions of people around you. People with good Emotional Intelligence are also able to express their emotions in a healthy and controlled way, which improves stress, communication and relationships with others.

The most common signs of Emotional Intelligence are:


  • Self-management - The ability to control impulsive feelings and behaviors, manage your emotions in healthy ways, take initiative, follow through on commitments, and adapt to changing circumstances.
  • Self-awareness - Recognising your own emotions and how they affect your thoughts and behavior. Knowing your strengths and weaknesses, and having self-confidence.
  • Social awareness - Being empathetic. You understand the emotions, needs, and concerns of other people, pick up on emotional cues, feel comfortable socially, and recognise the power dynamics in a group or organisation.
  • Relationship management - Knowing how to develop and maintain good relationships, communicate clearly, inspire and influence others, work well in a team, and manage conflict.

How is EQ different to IQ? EQ (Emotional Quotient) is the complete opposite to IQ (Intelligence Quotient). EQ refers to your ability to recognise and express emotions for yourself and others, whereas IQ refers to someone's intellectual ability eg. Problem solving. EQ is able to be worked on and improved with self-awareness, however IQ is thought to be more challenging to improve.

Why is EQ important? Emotional Intelligence is an extremely desirable skill and is often deemed more important than IQ overall. People with good Emotional Intelligence often have social skills and the ability to manage their emotions well which means they'll not only be really balanced individuals, they'll also be great in both co-operative and stressful situations.
